ON THE EMENDATION OF TIMES, BOOK V. 371

our plan. For there are also other [κοσμοποιηταί] "cosmographers" who have devised a new paradise. But death has begrudged us their splendid work. In Arabic Geography there are two rivers by the name of Gihon [جيحون]: one is that which the Greeks call Cydnus. For it is said to wash the city called Mamistra and to discharge into the Roman Sea, that is, the Issic Gulf. Now Mamistra is Tarsus. The other Gihon is a vast river, one of the six greatest of the whole world, whose names are these, as set down by that most learned Geographer himself: the Nile; Degilah, that is, the Tigris; Phrath, that is, the Euphrates; Mahran, the river of the Sindi, the Ganges; Bagnun of the Chinese; Gihon of Corasan.

ON THE FLOOD.

That from the creation of things to the Flood 1656 completed years elapsed is as certain—on the basis of the summaries of the years in which the births and deaths of Adam and his descendants were recorded by Moses—as anything that no one has hitherto doubted. The Flood therefore fell in the year 1657 from the creation of the world; in the year 2420 of the Julian Period, in solar cycle XIII, lunar cycle VII. Its neomenia in Libra [Zygonos] at 7h 18′ 12″, on October 13. Marchesvvan 5, 20h 349, feria VI (Friday), October 19, of the Jewish year 1468. 1656 completed years make 13 periods of the Persian Neuruz, which the Hebrews called [חלדים] Heladim, that is, ages.
