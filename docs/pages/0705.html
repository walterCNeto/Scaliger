English

that is, some celebrated Easter in the month of March, others on the 9th of the Kalends of May. But Aimoin also notes that he died in the year 741, so that there is no room for doubt. The year of that memorable battle, in which so many thousands of Saracens were slain by Martel on the plains of Tours, corresponds to the year of Christ 725, the ninth year of Charles Martel. This Bede indicates clearly, for he lived at the time these events occurred. "In the year," he says, "seven hundred twenty-five, at which time a most grievous plague of Saracens was devastating Gaul with wretched slaughter, and they themselves, not long after, were paying in that same province fitting penalties for their perfidy." Therefore it was not in the year 730, as Sigebert wrongly states (as in many other matters), that this defeat occurred. It is remarkable that the country people still preserve the memory of this event as though it were recent. The place, moreover, is called Saint Martin the Fair. The year 107 of the Hegira began in that year of Christ on May 19, feria VII (Saturday), solar cycle VI, lunar IV. Wherefore, without any doubt, that battle was fought in the year of Christ 725, year 106 of the Hegira, sixteen years after the Saracens, under the leadership of Moses son of Nuzir, crossed from Africa into Spain. The author of the Arabic Geography, a very good one, writes that this Musa had with him a most vigorous commander by the name of Tark, or Tarik, of whom John Leo also makes mention in his Africa; from whom the mountain nearest the Strait of Cadiz was called Gibal Tarik or Gibal Tark. For when he was speaking of the city which he calls [والجزيرة جزيرة الخضر] "Island of Chadhar," he adds: [Arabic text] "The first of the cities subdued in Spain was the green island" (that is, Gezirath alhadra. Roderic, book III, chap. XIX), "at the beginning of the Muslims (that is, when the Muhammadan religion was arising), and this in the ninetieth year of the Hegira. hoc est, alii mense Martio, alii IX Kal. Mai celebrarunt Pascha. Sed & Aimoinus anno 741 obiisse notat, ut dubitationi locus non sit. Annus autem memorabilis illius pugnae, qua tot millia Sarracenorum caesa a Martello sunt in campestribus Turonii, competit anno Christi 725, Caroli autem Martelli nono. quod Beda luculente indicat, qui eo tempore viuebat, quo haec acciderunt. Anno, inquit, septingentesimo vicesimo quinto, quo tempore grauissima Sarracenorum lues Gallias misera cade vastabat, & ipsi non multo post in eadem prouincia dignas suae perfidiae poenas luebant. Non igitur anno 730, ut perperam Sigebertus, sicut alia multa, haec clades contigit. Mirum est adhuc rusticos eius rei tanquam nuperae memoriam conseruare. Locus autem dicitur S. Martinus bellus. Annus Hegirae CVII caepit eo anno Christi, XIX Mai, feria VII, cyclo Solis VI, Lunae IV. Quare absque ullo dubio praelium illud commissum anno Christi DCCXXV, Hegirae CVI, sexdecim annis postquam Sarraceni duce Mose filio Nutzir ex Africa in Hispaniam traiecerunt. Notas do tradutor: Página dedicada à cronologia da Batalha de Tours (Poitiers) e à conquista árabe da Hispânia. Scaliger sincroniza o ano 725 d.C. com o 9º ano de Carlos Martel e com os anos 106-107 da Hégira. Cita Beda, Aimoíno, Sigeberto (refutado), o 'Geógrafo Árabe' (provavelmente al-Idrisi ou fonte similar), João Leão Africano e Rodrigo de Toledo. Os trechos árabes tratam da conquista de Algeciras (al-Jazira al-Khadra, 'ilha verde') e da etimologia de Gibraltar (Gibal Tarik).
