Quantas vezes leio os escritos dos Judeus, ocorre-me admirar como método tão perfeito pôde escapar a homens que, quase de propósito, parecem professar ignorância de todas as coisas boas. Seu epilogismo, contudo, no decurso do tempo se mostra um pouco mais longo que o justo em progresso lunar. O epilogismo da sizígia judaica é de 29 dias, 12 horas, 793 momentos — os quais 793 momentos equivalem a 44' 3" 20"' sexagesimais. Mas o verdadeiro epilogismo astronômico é de 12 hor. 44' 3" 11"'. Pois a medida do ciclo astronômico é menor que a do ciclo judaico. Por isso, subtraído o ciclo astronômico do ciclo juliano, restará excesso maior do que [se resultasse] do [ciclo] judaico

Further, in an Embolismic year, such as this 5356 now running toward its end, the Tekuphah of Tisri will fall in Marcheshvan, and the Tekuphah of Tebeth in Schebat. Gather therefore the excesses corresponding to 281 cycles and 16 completed years. By the perpetual rule deduce the days 12.20.204. Add the hours and moments of the new moon 2.12.700. The result is 31 days, 3 hours, 0. Therefore the autumnal Tekuphah fell three hours into the night following the 31st day from the neomenia of Tisri—that is, on the second night of Marcheshvan, at the beginning of the fifth feria. Having obtained the Tekuphah of Tisri, you will obtain the following one by adding to the former 91 days and 7½ hours. Thus in the proposed year 5356, from the neomenia of Tisri there will be 122 days and 10½ hours. Wherefore the winter Tekuphah will fall in the night following the 122nd day from the neomenia of Tisri. It was an abundant year. Tisri, Marcheshvan, Casleu: 90 days; combined with the 29 days of Tebet, the total is 119 days: from which, subtracting 122, there remain 3 days and 10½ hours of the month of Schebat. Therefore the winter Tekuphah occurred at 10½ hour of the night following the 3rd day of Schebat; and thus the Tekuphah occurred on the fourth night of Schebat, on the fifth feria. These things are most ingenious; yet their causes are unknown to the very artificers of the Computus, who in place of such causes set before us fables, that like the spectator in Plautus we may be sated with tales. Yet no one moderately versed in these matters will deny that the method of this Lunar Computus is most acute and elegant. As often as I read the writings of the Jews, I marvel how so perfect a method could have come forth from men who almost purposely seem to profess ignorance of all good things. Their epilogism, however, is found in the course of time to run a little longer than the true lunar progress. The epilogism of the Jewish syzygy is 29 days, 12 hours, 793 moments—which 793 moments amount to 44' 3" 20"' sexagesimal. But the true astronomical epilogism is 12 hr. 44' 3" 11"'. The difference is 0' 0" 9"', which, multiplied cubically by the syzygies of one complete cycle, yields 0' 35" 15"'. The Jewish cycle is 6939 days, 16 hr. 595—that is, 16 hr. 33' 3" 20"'. Subtracting 0' 35" 15"', there remain 6939 days, 16 hr. 32' 28" 5"', the measure of the astronomical cycle. The Julian cycle is 6939 days, 18 hr. 0. The difference between the Julian and the astronomical cycle is 1 hr. 27' 31" 55"', an excess greater on the Julian side. For the measure of the astronomical cycle is less than that of the Jewish cycle. Tekupha igitur autumnalis fuit post horas tres, nocte, quae sequuta est 31 diem a neomenia Tisri, id est, nocte secunda Marcheschvvan, feria quinta ineunte.
