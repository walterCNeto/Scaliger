Dessas palavras de Josefo também é manifesto que a denominação dos meses macedônicos estava em uso entre os próprios judeus, isto é, nos contratos em que usavam da era Macedônica.

SOBRE O PERÍODO DOS SIRO-MACEDÔNIOS ALEXANDRINOS.

Bem fez, na verdade, Calipo, que primeiro detectou, indicou e emendou o excesso de um dia nos 76 anos metônicos. Mas, para instituir a economia dos meses cavos, aguardar até que se transcorressem 64 dias — isto é, na verdade, fazer violência ao próprio movimento do astro. the reason given is that he feared that, if the Macedonian months were cited as they had been transposed by Calippus, this would cause confusion for the reader. Which indeed could not be avoided. But let us restore to these Eclipses their proper months. In year 54 of the Attic Calippic period, Hecatombaeon fell on July 12, on the letter D. It was therefore feria IV, Solar cycle V. This, combined with the characteristic of Boedromion, yields feria VII for the new moon of Boedromion, on September 9. The Eclipse therefore occurred on the 14th of Boedromion for the Athenians, but for the Macedonians on the 1st of the new Gorpiaeus. Again, the fourth feria, combined with the fifth characteristic of Elaphebolion, the septenary being discarded, yields the second feria of Elaphebolion, or of the new Macedonian Dystros, on March 5. Therefore the new moon of Elaphebolion, or of Dystros, fell on March 5, and consequently the Eclipse on the 15th of Elaphebolion, or of Dystros, in year 54 Attic, 55 Macedonian. Finally, Hecatombaeon of year 55 Attic and Macedonian fell on the Kalends of July, feria one: which, combined with the 3rd characteristic of Boedromion, produces feria 4 for the new moon of Attic Boedromion, but of the new Gorpiaeus on August 29. Therefore the Eclipse occurred on the 14th of Boedromion, or Gorpiaeus. Again, in year 37 of the third period, the Moon was eclipsed in year 607 of Nabonassar, on the 2nd day of Tybi, followed by the 3rd, feria 3, in year 4573 of the Julian Period. The time was January 27. The beginning of that year fell in Solar cycle 8. The new moon of Hecatombaeon was on July 20, feria two; which, combined with the 2nd characteristic of Gamelion, yields feria four for the new moon of Gamelion on January 14, the Dominical letter being DC. Therefore the Eclipse occurred on the 14th of Gamelion or of the new Audynaeus. As to what we have noted about the Tetraeteric months observed among the Macedonians, that is, that the Syro-Macedonians used a double year, of [τριακονθημέρων] "thirty-day" months and Lunar months, as the Athenians were accustomed — besides other things we could adduce to strengthen our opinion — the abundant witness Josephus will confirm, who writes thus: [ὑπερβερεταίου τῇ δεκάτῃ κατὰ σελήνην ἱσταμένου] "on the tenth of Hyperberetaeus according to the waxing moon." He said Hyperberetaeus [κατὰ σελήνην] "according to the moon," because there was a [ὑπερβερεταῖος τετραετηρικὸς τριακονθήμερος μὴ κατὰ σελήνην] "tetraeteric thirty-day Hyperberetaeus not according to the moon." So too Thucydides said [νουμηνίαν κατὰ σελήνην] "new moon according to the moon," because, evidently, there was another [νουμηνία μὴ κατὰ σελήνην] "new moon not according to the moon," namely, the tetraeteric one of the [τριακονθημέρου] "thirty-day" month. From these words of Josephus it is also manifest that the designation of the Macedonian months was in use among the Jews themselves, namely in contracts in which they employed the Macedonian era.

ON THE PERIOD OF THE ALEXANDRIAN SYRO-MACEDONIANS.

Calippus indeed did well, who was the first to detect, indicate, and correct the excess of one day in 76 Metonic years. But in order to establish the economy of the hollow months, to wait until 64 days have elapsed — this is truly to do violence to the very motion of the luminary itself. Ex quibus etiam verbis Iosephi manifestum est ipsis Iudaeis mensium Macedonicorum appellationem in usu fuisse in contractibus scilicet, in quibus utebantur aera Macedonica.
