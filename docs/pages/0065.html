English

...was encircled by Nebuchadnezzar, as Daniel IV, 27 attests. And many other matters besides, in the elucidation of which the labor is pleasant and the fruit most abundant. But no less usefulness is gathered from the fragment of Menander of Ephesus, preserved in the same Josephus, who, beginning from Hiram, or King Iromus, who supplied cedar wood to Solomon, down to Pygmalion, brother of Dido, enumerates all the Kings of the Sidonians and Tyrians. The first is [εἴρωμος, חִירָם], son of [ἀβιβάλου, אֲבִי־בַעַל]. The second, son of Iromus, [βαλεάζαρος, בַּעַל־עֶזֶר], reigned seven years. His son [ἀβδάσαρτος, עֶבֶד־עַשְׁתֹּרֶת], nine years; after he was killed by the nurse's sons, the eldest of the nurse's sons, [ἀνώνυμος] ("nameless"), reigned two years. After him [ἄσαρτος ὁ Δελαιαστάρτου, עַשְׁתֹּרֶת דְּלִי עַשְׁתֹּרֶת], twelve years. His brother [ἀσέρυμος, חִצְרִים] succeeded him, nine years; when he was killed by his brother Phelles, Phelles himself [Φέλης, פֶּלֶא] reigned eight months. After he was killed, [εἰθώβαλος, אֶת־בַעַל], Priest of Astarte, thirty-two years. His successor [βαδέζωρος, בְּדִי עֶזֶר], six years. His son [μάτγηνος, מָט גָּאוֹן], nine. He was succeeded by [Φυγμαλίων, פִּתְנַס־עֶלְיוֹן], who reigned forty-seven years. Among these we see Iromus, who, as we said, supplied cedars to Solomon, and likewise Ithobaal, whose daughter Jezebel was taken as wife by Omri, King of Samaria (1 Kings XVI, 31). From the death of Iromus — which Josephus writes occurred in the twelfth year after the temple was built — to the seventh year of Pygmalion, in which Dido founded Carthage, Josephus writes that 155 years and eight months elapsed. But the reckoning escapes him. For he includes the twelve years of Hiram, which are excluded by hypothesis, and besides the full 47 years of Pygmalion, whereas forty-one ought to be excepted. Thus from the death of Iromus to the founding of Carthage there were only 103 years. But concerning all these Kings, and their years, and what fruit can be gathered from reading this fragment, we shall speak more fully elsewhere, in the little book of Fragments. In Tertullian's Apologeticum, chap. XIX, one reads "Hieronymus Phoenix Rex Tyri." Read "Hiromus Phoenix." But in the meantime something must be said about Carthage. Carthage was destroyed in the year 606 from the founding of Rome, 4567 of the Julian period, 737 years after it had been founded by Dido, according to Solinus. Deducting 737 from 4567, there remains the seventh year of Ithobaal, 3830 in the Julian Period. Omri, King of Samaria, son-in-law of Ithobaal, began to reign in year 578 from the Exodus, that is, 3795 of the Julian period. From the beginning of Ithobaal to the seventh year of Pygmalion there are 53 completed years; deducting these from 3830, there remains year 3777 in the Julian period, the first year of Ithobaal's reign. Flags de incerteza (pontos para revisão humana)
  • Transliterações gregas e hebraicas dos nomes dos reis de Tiro foram preservadas conforme o original; algumas formas hebraicas (como חִצְרִים, פִּתְנַס־עֶלְיוֹן) são conjecturas etimológicas de Scaliger e podem não corresponder a formas hebraicas canônicas.
  • Na linha 24, o latim diz 'totos 47 Pygmalionis, cum quadraginta et unus excipiendi sint' — a tradução segue o sentido: de 47 anos, apenas 41 devem ser excluídos (ou seja, 6 anos do reinado até o sétimo ano de Pigmalião devem ser contados).
Notas do tradutor: Página da seção Prolegomena, continuando discussão cronológica sobre os reis de Tiro a partir do fragmento de Menandro de Éfeso citado por Josefo, e sobre a datação da fundação e destruição de Cartago. Números-chave confirmados: 4567 (periodus Juliana da destruição de Cartago), 737 (anos desde a fundação), 3830 (sétimo ano de Itobaal), 3795 (início do reinado de Onri), 3777 (primeiro ano de Itobaal), 155 (cálculo errôneo de Josefo), 103 (cálculo correto de Scaliger).
