SOBRE O PERÍODO DOS BITÍNIOS.

No ano 840 de Nabonassar, completo o dia 11 de Tybi, Agrippa, o astrólogo, observou na Bitínia a Lua conjunta com a [estrela] austral seguinte das Vergílias (Plêiades), o que era o décimo segundo ano de Domiciano, dia 7 de Metrôo segundo os bitínios. A data: 29 de novembro, no ano 92 da era cristã vulgar. Portanto a neomênia de [Μητρῴου] Metrôo correspondia a 23 de novembro; e, consequentemente, o mês estival a 26 de julho. Era o ano ifiteu 868, e o 32 do décimo segundo período olímpico. Nele, o mês estival coincide três vezes com 26 de julho, nos anos 13, 30, 47. Mas, como o ano proposto fosse o quarto de uma Olimpíada, também o ano bitínio deveria ser ou o quarto da tetraetéride bitínica, ou o segundo: pois de outro modo os anos olímpicos não são usados em outros períodos. Contudo, nenhum quarto ano da tetraetéride olímpica tem início em 26 de julho. Será portanto o ano 32 do período olímpico, mas o 30

English

understand: Unless, as I believe, it be the [ἑκατομβαιών] "Hecatombaion" Callippic Lunar. And indeed I do not doubt that this is true. Moreover, the Syrians have maintained this epoch down to the present day from the Julian month of October, on account of the old Hyperberetaeus; and they call this epoch Aliscandria, that is, Alexandreia. But the Arabs and certain other nations long reckoned it from Elul, that is, September, on account of the Constantinian Indiction. And this epoch is called by them [תאריח די אלקרנין / تاريخ ذي القرنين] Terich dilkarnain, that is, [ἐποχὴ τοῦ δικέρωτος] "the epoch of the Two-Horned One." For they call Alexander [δικέρωτα] "the Two-Horned," as will be stated below. As to what Plutarch writes in his Alexander, that the Macedonian month [λῷον] "Loos" is the same as Hecatombaion, this will be true if Loos is understood as tetraeteric and Hecatombaion, on the other hand, as lunar and Callippic. For in the second year of the Alexandrian period, the lunar or Callippic Hecatombaion occupied July, whereas Loos invaded August. For the tetraeteric months lag behind the lunar ones in the second, third, and fourth years of the tetraeteris.

ON THE PERIOD OF THE BITHYNIANS.

In the year 840 from Nabonassar, after the 11th of Tybi had been completed, Agrippa the astrologer observed in Bithynia the Moon conjoined with the following southern star of the Pleiades (Vergiliae), which was the twelfth year of Domitian, the 7th day of Metroos according to the Bithynians. The date: 29 November, in the common year of Christ 92. Therefore the new moon of [Μητρῴου] Metroos fell on 23 November; and consequently the summer month on 26 July. It was the Iphitean year 868, and the 32nd of the twelfth Olympic period. In this period the summer month coincides three times with 26 July, in the years 13, 30, 47. But since the year in question was the fourth of an Olympiad, the Bithynian year too must have been either the fourth of the Bithynian tetraeteris, or the second: for otherwise the Olympic years are not employed in other periods. But no fourth year of the Olympic tetraeteris begins on 26 July. It will therefore be the 32nd year of the Olympic period, but the 30th

telligere: Nisi, quod credo, fuerit is ἑκατομβαιών Calippicus Lunaris. Et sane non dubito, quin verum sit. Porro hanc epocham Syri tenuerunt ad hunc usque diem a mense Octobri Iuliano, propter veterem Hyperberetaeum: eamque epocham Aliscandria vocant, id est Alexandream. Sed Arabes, et quaedam aliae nationes diu usurparunt ab Elul, id est Septembri, propter Indictionem Constantinianam. Eaque epoche vocata est illis תאריח די אלקרנין تاريخ ذي القرنين Terich dilkarnain, hoc est ἐποχὴ τοῦ δικέρωτος. Alexandrum enim vocant δικέρωτα, ut infra dicetur. Quod autem Plutarchus in Alexandro scribit λῷον Macedonicum mensem esse eundem cum Hecatombaeone, hoc verum fuerit, si λῷος Tetraetericus, Hecatombaeon vero Lunaris, et Calippicus intelligatur. Nam secundo anno periodi Alexandreae Hecatombaeon Lunaris vel Calippicus occupabat Iulium, λῷος vero invadebat Augustum. Nam menses Tetraeterici sunt tardiores Lunaribus in annis secundo, tertio, quarto Tetraeteridos.

DE PERIODO BITHYNORUM.

Anno a Nabonassaro 840, Tybi 11 absoluto, Agrippa astrologus in Bithynia observavit Lunam iunctam cum australi succedente Vergiliarum, qui erat annus XII Domitiani, Metroi secundum Bithynos die VII. Tempus, XXIX Novembris, anno Christi vulgari 92: Ergo neomenia Μητρῴου congruebat vicesimae tertiae Novembris: et proinde mensis aestivus 26 Iulii. Erat annus Iphiteus 868, et periodi duodecimae Olympicae 32. In qua mensis aestivus ter competit vicesimae sextae Iulii, in annis 13, 30, 47. Sed cum annus propositus esset quartus Olympiadis, annum quoque Bithynicum aut quartum fuisse oportet Tetraeteridis Bithynicae, aut secundum: quod aliter anni Olympici non usurpantur in aliis periodis.
