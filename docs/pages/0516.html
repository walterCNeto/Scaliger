SOBRE O INÍCIO DE PTOLOMEU FILADELFO E SUA ERA CELESTE.

Tudo quanto os historiadores escreveram sobre os primeiros Ptolomeus e sobre os reis da Síria, fizeram-no mais por certa conjectura do que com [πληροφορείᾳ] "plena certeza", para usar a palavra apostólica. A Ptolomeu Lago, que primeiro invadiu o Egito e primeiro ali fundou um vastíssimo império, e deixou aos seus descendentes, juntamente com o império, o cognome hereditário dos Ptolomeus, atribuem-se 40 anos. E com razão: pois começou a reinar imediatamente após a morte de Alexandre. Mas se, desde a morte de Alexandre, se deve conceder algum intervalo aos combates e aos eventos de guerra que ocorreram entre os próceres do exército macedônico, não parecerá que tenham tomado o império imediatamente após a morte de Alexandre, ao menos de modo a parecerem dignos do título régio. Isso creremos ter acontecido também a Seleuco Nicátor, se se pode dar crédito a Eusébio e outros antigos, os quais no 12º ano após a morte de Alexandre, isto é, no primeiro ano dos Siromacedônios Alexandrino

Zygon 1.16.7, on September 27th, as above, because of the bissextile, when it ought to have been on the 28th in a common year. In the Jewish year 3525, Marcheshvan 14, whose character is 6.5.375, October 16th. The Chaldean month therefore agrees with the Callippic, and with the Jewish, both ancient and modern. Finally, in the 519th year of Nabonassar, [Τυβί ιϛ] "Tybi 16," the 82nd of the Chaldeans, [Ξανθικοῦ ζ] "Xanthikos 7," the mean Sun at 6°10' of Pisces, 4485 of the Julian Period, Kalends of March, Wednesday, solar cycle 5, World year 3721, Ichthyonos VIII. Zygon 3.8.52', September 23rd. But according to Ptolemy Zygon would have fallen on September 27th, in a common year, which eight years earlier, in a common year, would have fallen on the Kalends of October. You see manifest [ἀτοπίαν] "absurdity." In the Jewish year 3532, second Adar 7, whose character is 5.12.178, February 24th, bissextile day. The same also in the 25th year of the second Callippic period. For it was [Ἐλαφηβολιῶνος ἑβδόμη] "the seventh of Elaphebolion." Therefore the things we said in the second book, in the chapter on the Chaldean year, must be corrected. For in vain did we wish to reconcile this passage. For there is an error in Ptolemy's codex: [Ξανθικοῦ] Ε instead of Ζ. It could easily have been a slip from E to Z. Nor do I doubt it is so.

ON THE BEGINNING OF PTOLEMY PHILADELPHUS AND HIS CELESTIAL ERA.

Whatever historians have written about the earlier Ptolemies and the kings of Syria, they did it rather by a certain conjecture than with [πληροφορείᾳ] "full certainty," to use the Apostolic word. To Ptolemy Lagus, who first invaded Egypt, and first founded there a most vast empire, and left to his descendants, along with the empire, the hereditary cognomen of the Ptolemies, they attribute 40 years. Rightly indeed: for he began to reign immediately after the death of Alexander. But if, from the death of Alexander, some interval must be granted to the battles and events of war that occurred among the chief men of the Macedonian army, they will not appear to have seized the empire immediately after the death of Alexander, at least in such a way as to seem worthy of the royal title. We shall believe that this also happened to Seleucus Nicator, if any credit is to be given to Eusebius and other ancients, who in the 12th year after the death of Alexander, that is, in the first year of the Syromacedonians, Alexandrian

DE INITIO PTOLEMAEI PHILADELPHI ET EIVS AERA CAELESTI.

Quicquid historici de prioribus Ptolemaeis, & regibus Syriae scripserunt, id coniectura potius quadam, quam πληροφορείᾳ, ut verbum Apostolicum usurpem, fecerunt. Ptolemaeo Lagi, qui primus Aegyptum invasit, & primus in ea imperium amplissimum condidit, ac posteris suis Ptolemaeorum cognomen hereditarium cum imperio reliquit, annos XL attribuunt. bene sane: siquidem statim a morte Alexandri imperare caepit. Sin autem a morte Alexandri intervallum aliquod dandum proeliis, & eventibus belli, quae inter proceres exercitus Macedonici intercesserunt, non statim a morte Alexandri imperium arripuisse videbuntur, saltem ut nomine regio digni videantur. quod etiam Seleuco Nicatori accidisse crediderimus, si qua fides Eusebio & aliis veteribus, qui XII anno post obitum Alexandri, hoc est, anno primo Syromacedonum Alexandreo
