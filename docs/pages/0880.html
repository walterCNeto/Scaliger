English

INDEX.

First page of the alphabetical index, containing entries from A through P (partial). It lists proper names (Agrippa the astrologer, Alaric, Alboin king of the Lombards, Alexander the Great, Alexander the martyr, etc.) and technical chronological terms. The longest entry is ANNVS (YEAR), with many subdivisions: Jewish abundant year, Year of Adda the Hebrew doctor, Egyptian Actiac year, Egyptian year of Nabonassar, Egyptian wandering year, Greater and lesser equable year of the Greeks, Year of the Albanians, of the Aricinians, Chaldean Alexandrian year, Syro-Macedonian Alexandrian year, of the Armenians, Astric year, Old Attic year (beginning at winter solstice), more recent Attic year (from the solstice), Attic Julian year, Egyptian and Roman year of the Augusti, Celestial year, New most emended celestial year designated by the author, Chaldean celestial year with parapegm, Muhammadan celestial year, Celestial year with hemerology, Callippic year (Prytany, tetraeteric, solar), Egyptian canicular year, Jewish cautious year, Chaldean Alexandrian year, Jewish common year, Caesarean year of Confusion, Year of Confusion of 444 days, Cynic year, Jewish defective year, Year of Democritus, Diocletianic year, Embolimic year, Emended year of three kinds (with Greek terms referring to heliacal and enneadecaeteric isemerine cycles), Year of the ancient Gauls, Gazene year purely Actiac, Year of the ancient Germans, Year of the Greeks (with reference to 262 days), Year of Grace, Year of Harpalus, Equable year of the ancient Hebrews, Year of the Hegira, Fictitious year of Herodotus, Hierophantic or Julian year, Year of Hipparchus, Julian year of the Spaniards, year in which Jesus Christ was called back from Egypt, Year of Yazdegerd, Year of the Indians, Year of Jupiter, Modern or recent Jewish year (a most artificial matter, with double beginning), Julian year (foundation of the author's institution), Julian year of the Egyptians, Athenians, ancient Damascenes, more recent Damascenes, Spaniards, Jews, Syro-Greeks and Antiochenes, Samaritans, Year of the Moon (in use among all Indians, and from what time in use among the Jews), Great Year of Democritus, Hipparchus, Meton, Oenopides, Philolaus, Year of Mars, Year of Mercury, Metonic lunar year, Year of the Mexicans of 18 months, Year of Nabonassar, New year i.e. Paschal, New year, Year of Oenopides, Jewish ordinary year, Philippic year of Meton, Year of Philolaus, Roma[n — continues on next page].

Notas do tradutor: Página do índice alfabético (frontmatter posterior). A entrada ANNVS é especialmente rica e mostra o escopo comparativo da obra de Scaliger sobre todos os calendários conhecidos da Antiguidade. Termos gregos no item 'Emendatus triplicis generis' são técnicos da teoria do ciclo de 19 anos (enneadecaeterís) e do ano isemerino (equinocial).
