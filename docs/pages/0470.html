Hence the matter split almost into two factions, so that some Palilia are Catonian, others Varronian. In the Capitoline Fasti, the consulship of C. Claudius Nero and M. Livius Salinator II is placed in the 546th year of the City. Therefore the Consuls who immediately preceded them, M. Claudius Marcellus (for the 5th time) and T. Quinctius Crispinus, held office in the 545th year of the City, according to those Capitoline stones. But Livy says that under those consuls the Olympic games were celebrated, which no one would deny to have been the 143rd Olympiad, in the year 4506 of the Julian period. The Palilia therefore of those consuls fall in the fourth year of the 142nd Olympiad, that is, in the 568th year of Iphitus. Therefore Rome was founded in the 24th year of Iphitus, according to the Capitoline Fasti. And consequently the Palilia of the Capitoline Fasti are Catonian. Again, Livy writes that, during those days when the Consul L. Cornelius Scipio, brother of Africanus, set out for war, during the Apollinarian games, on the fifth day before the Ides of Quintilis, with a clear sky, the daylight was obscured at noon, the Moon having passed beneath the orb of the Sun. This eclipse without doubt occurred on the day before the Julian Ides of March, in the year 4524 of the Julian period, in the second year of the lunar cycle. But the Capitoline Fasti place the consuls L. Scipio Asiaticus and C. Laelius, son of Caius, grandson, in the 563rd year of the City. Therefore the City was founded in the year 3961 of the Julian period, as above, that is, in year 24 of Iphitus. But a knot remains. For Piso places the consulship of Scipio Asiaticus and of Laelius in the 516th year of the City. An interval of 33 consuls. But in Cassiodorus and in the Onufrian Fasti only 32. Hence one pair of Consuls would have escaped. But this is false. For the Capitoline Fasti place these two Consuls in the 595th year of the City, not in the 596th, as Piso, and Onufrius who followed him. Solinus follows the Capitoline Fasti in chapter two. He writes that, under the consulship of C. Pompeius Gallus and Q. Verannius, in the 801st year of the City, the 207th Olympiad was marked by public acts. The 207th Olympiad began in the year 4762 of the Julian period, in the twelfth year of the lunar cycle. Therefore 9 must be added to 801, so that, divided by 19, there remain 12. And consequently Rome was founded in the tenth year of the lunar cycle, in the year 3961 of the Julian period. And the old Chronologer in Clement: [ἀπὸ τῆς πρώτης Ὀλυμπιάδος, ἐπὶ τῆς Ῥώμης κτίσιν συνάγε[?] ἔτη εἴκοσι τέσσαρα] "from the first Olympiad to the founding of Rome they amount to twenty-four years." Therefore the Capitoline Fasti and Solinus follow the Catonian Palilia. But one must carefully beware not to take the current year of the City for the beginning one. This can impose even on the more prudent. For when it is said that those Consuls entered upon the magistracy on the Kalends of January of that year of the City, it can be doubted whether the flowing year, or one to be begun from the nearest Palilia, is to be understood.
