SOBRE A MORTE DE ARTAXERXES LONGÍMANO E O INÍCIO DE DARIO NOTO.

A Artaxerxes, cujo cognome foi [μακρόχειρ] "de mão longa", ou porque tinha uma das mãos maior, como atesta Plutarco, ou porque ambos os braços eram mais longos do que as proporções do corpo humano exigem, como diz Estrabão — a esse Artaxerxes, pois, Eusébio atribui 40 anos, e com mais acerto do que Ctésias, que lhe dá 42. Pois sua morte é remetida por Tucídides ao sétimo ano da guerra do Peloponeso, no início do inverno: isto é, no fim do ano 4288 do período juliano. Morreu, portanto, no quadragésimo ano de seu império. Ora, o ano 4289 é o primeiro de Dario Noto, que antes se chamava Ἄχος ou Ὦχος e que, após ascender ao império, por instituição e costume dos Persas, mudou de nome e passou a chamar-se Δαριαῖος. A autoridade é Ctésias, que escreveu sua [Περσικά] "História Pérsica" sob o filho dele, Artaxerxes. O início desse reinado se confirma tanto pelo momento da morte do próprio Artaxerxes pai quanto também pelo vigésimo ano da guerra do Peloponeso, o qual, tendo sido o décimo terceiro de Dario Noto, como escreve Tucídides, faz com que seu início dependa do sétimo ano ainda não terminado — isto é, do intervalo entre o inverno e a primavera. O fim de seu império deduz-se de Diodoro Sículo, que coloca sua morte no ano IV da Olimpíada 93, por volta do término da guerra do Peloponeso, o qual era o ano 27 desde a invasão de Arquidamo. Reinou, pois, 19 anos: o que também foi anotado por Eusébio. Esse tempo corresponde ao ano 4309 do período juliano. Again, since we lack the names of the Elean months, in the Olympic period — which was precisely the same as the Macedonian — we shall use the Macedonian names of the months, of which Hyperberetaeus is the one on which the Olympiad fell; this month the Syromacedonians later established as the first autumnal month. Therefore let the careful reader beware not to confuse the Olympic Hyperberetaeus of the Macedonians with the autumnal Hyperberetaeus of the Syromacedonians. Furthermore, Diodorus Siculus, from the capture of Ilium to the oligarchy imposed by Lysander, fixes 779 complete years. The year of the capture of Ilium is 3531. The year of the Lysandrean oligarchy is 4310. The difference is 779 years, exactly as Diodorus determined.

ON THE DEATH OF ARTAXERXES LONGIMANUS AND THE BEGINNING OF DARIUS NOTHUS.

To Artaxerxes, whose surname was [μακρόχειρ] "long-handed," either because he had one hand longer than the other, as Plutarch attests, or because both his arms were longer than the proportions of the human body require, as Strabo says — to this Artaxerxes, then, Eusebius attributes 40 years, and indeed more correctly than Ctesias, who gives him 42. For his death is placed by Thucydides in the seventh year of the Peloponnesian War, at the beginning of winter: that is, at the end of year 4288 of the Julian period. He therefore died in the fortieth year of his reign. And year 4289 is the first of Darius Nothus, who was previously called Ἄχος or Ὦχος, and who, after ascending to the throne, by the institution and custom of the Persians, changed his name and was called Δαριαῖος. The authority is Ctesias, who wrote his [Περσικά] "Persian History" under his son Artaxerxes. The beginning of this reign is confirmed both by the time of the death of Artaxerxes himself, the father, and also by the twentieth year of the Peloponnesian War, which, having been the thirteenth of Darius Nothus, as Thucydides writes, causes its beginning to depend upon the seventh year not yet completed — that is, upon the interval from winter to spring. The end of his reign is gathered from Diodorus Siculus, who places his death in the fourth year of the 93rd Olympiad, around the end of the Peloponnesian War, which was the 27th year from the invasion of Archidamus. He reigned, therefore, 19 years: which has also been noted by Eusebius. This time corresponds to year 4309 of the Julian period. Eusebius and Justin agree with Diodorus, who compares the time of this Darius's death with the capture of Athens and the victory of Lysander. But when the same Justin places the exile of Dionysius, tyrant of Sicily, in the same year, he errs both as to time and as to person. Artaxerxi, cui cognomen μακρόχειρ fuit, quod alteram manum longiorem haberet, ut auctor est Plutarchus, vel quod utrunque brachium longius, quam commensus corporis humani postulat, ut ait Strabo: ei ergo Artaxerxi XL annos attribuit Eusebius, & quidem melius, quam Ctesias, qui dat illi XLII. Nam eius obitus a Thucydide reiicitur in annum VII belli Peloponnesiaci, principio hiemis: hoc est, exitu anni periodi Iulianae 4288. Decessit ergo anno quadragesimo imperij. Annus vero 4289 est primus Darij Nothi, qui antea Ἄχος siue Ὦχος dictus, postquam ad imperium accessit, ex instituto, & consuetudine Persarum, mutato nomine Δαριαῖος dictus. Auctor Ctesias, qui sub eius filio Artaxerxe Περσικά sua scripsit.
