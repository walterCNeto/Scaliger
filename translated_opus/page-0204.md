SOBRE O ANO NOVO DOS JUDEUS.

Já era manifesta uma antecipação [προέμπτωσις] de dois dias no período selêucida judaico, quando, após o império de Constantino César, os romanos passaram a contar seus tempos pelas Indicções constantinianas, e isso a partir de 24 de setembro. Pois então haviam transcorrido mais de duas períodos hiparqueanas desde o início dos Selêucidas, e por isso a Lua havia antecipado em dois dias sua posição original. Com efeito, no ano de Cristo 344, que era o 656 dos Selêucidas, e o quadragésimo oitavo após duas períodos hiparqueanas, o Tisri alexandrino dos judeus caía em 26 de setembro, feira 4ª; quando, entretanto, pelo cômputo epilogístico hodierno, o caráter da neomenia 2, 4, 204 fixaria o início do mês em 24 de setembro. Eis o desvio de dois dias. Pois os judeus ainda não haviam perturbado o cômputo calípico em sua sede, mas até então o haviam conservado íntegro. ON THE NEW YEAR OF THE JEWS.

An anticipation [προέμπτωσις] of two days was already manifest in the Jewish Seleucid period, when, after the empire of Constantine Caesar, the Romans began to reckon their times by the Constantinian Indictions, and that from September 24. For by then more than two Hipparchian periods had flowed from the beginning of the Seleucids, and therefore the Moon had anticipated its original position by two days. For in the year of Christ 344, which was the 656th of the Seleucids and the forty-eighth after two Hipparchian periods, the Alexandrian Tisri of the Jews fell on September 26, weekday 4; whereas according to the present-day epilogistic reckoning the neomenia-character 2, 4, 204 would set the start of the month on September 24. Here is the two-day discrepancy. For the Jews had not yet disturbed the Callippic computation in its seat, but had preserved it intact until then. At length, the error being belatedly recognized, they abandoned the reasonings of the Callippic period and took refuge in safer defenses, calling astrology [astronomy] to their aid; to whose scrutiny, as they weighed the reckonings of the months, they detected the discrepancy which arises from the succession of enneadecaeteric cycles.
