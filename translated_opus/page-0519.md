## English

ON THE BEGINNING OF SIMON, ETHNARCH OF THE JEWS.

The Jews first abandoned the era of contracts, which they had reckoned from Tisri only in public and legal acts, when Simon Hyrcanus undertook the high priesthood at Jerusalem, in the 170th year of the Alexandrian era. For then, for the first time, it began to be subscribed in legal acts not, as before, IN SUCH AND SUCH A YEAR OF THE GREEKS, but rather IN SUCH A YEAR OF HYRCANUS, ETHNARCH OF THE JEWS AND HIGH PRIEST. The author of the first book of Maccabees, chap. XIII, 41, and after him Josephus, [attests this]. In Perek Rosh Hashana, from the opinion of Rab Aha bar Huna [Hebrew text], that is: "On the third of Tisri the mention in contracts was abolished, of the edict by which the kings of Greece had decreed that they should not make mention of the name of God in their mouth. But after the kingdom of the Hasmoneans prevailed and they conquered them, they decreed that the name of God should be mentioned even in contracts. And thus they used to write: IN SUCH AND SUCH A YEAR OF JOHANAN, PRIEST OF THE GREAT SUPREME GOD. Hearing this, the sages said: Tomorrow someone will pay his debt, and the bond will be found cast into the dung-heap. So they abolished it, and appointed that day as a festival." It is true indeed that the year and the name of Simon were added, but together with the year of the Greeks. 1 Maccabees XIV, 27. For there is the legal act. In the same year 170 of the Greeks, 171 of the Jews, 23 Ijar, a solemnity was instituted for the expulsion of the Macedonian garrison from the citadel. It was the 18th year of the third Alexandrian period. Tisri 28 September, feria VII (Saturday), with Marcheshvan full. Therefore Nisan feria 3 (Tuesday), 25 March. Ijar 24, April, Thursday, solar cycle VIII, lunar cycle XII, year 4572 of the Julian Period. And accordingly 23 Ijar, 16 May, feria VI (Friday): year of the world 3808, Tauron 23. Therefore the neomenias of Tauron and civil Ijar coincided, indeed also the prytany of Thargelion of year 36 of the third Callippic period. Zygon. 6. II. 17, 27 September, Jewish year 3619, Ijar [κατὰ σελήνην] "according to the moon" 25: whose character is 3. II. 752, 22 April. Hence I think the true reckoning of the civil years of that age begins. For Josephus says that Jerusalem was taken by Pompey in the third month, [ἐν νηστείᾳ] "during the fast." Tamuz is indeed the third from Ijar. Elsewhere the same writer calls Adar the eleventh month, and Tebeth the ninth, namely from Ijar.
