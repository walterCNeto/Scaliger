SOBRE O PERÍODO DOS BITÍNIOS.

No ano 840 de Nabonassar, completo o dia 11 de Tybi, Agrippa, o astrólogo, observou na Bitínia a Lua conjunta com a [estrela] austral seguinte das Vergílias (Plêiades), o que era o décimo segundo ano de Domiciano, dia 7 de Metrôo segundo os bitínios. Era o ano ifiteu 868, e o 32 do décimo segundo período olímpico. Nele, o mês estival coincide três vezes com 26 de julho, nos anos 13, 30, 47. ## English

Moreover, the Syrians have maintained this epoch down to the present day from the Julian month of October, on account of the old Hyperberetaeus; and they call this epoch Aliscandria, that is, Alexandreia. But the Arabs and certain other nations long reckoned it from Elul, that is, September, on account of the Constantinian Indiction. And this epoch is called by them [תאריח די אלקרנין / تاريخ ذي القرنين] Terich dilkarnain, that is, [ἐποχὴ τοῦ δικέρωτος] "the epoch of the Two-Horned One." For they call Alexander [δικέρωτα] "the Two-Horned," as will be stated below. As to what Plutarch writes in his Alexander, that the Macedonian month [λῷον] "Loos" is the same as Hecatombaion, this will be true if Loos is understood as tetraeteric and Hecatombaion, on the other hand, as lunar and Callippic. For in the second year of the Alexandrian period, the lunar or Callippic Hecatombaion occupied July, whereas Loos invaded August. For the tetraeteric months lag behind the lunar ones in the second, third, and fourth years of the tetraeteris.

ON THE PERIOD OF THE BITHYNIANS.

In the year 840 from Nabonassar, after the 11th of Tybi had been completed, Agrippa the astrologer observed in Bithynia the Moon conjoined with the following southern star of the Pleiades (Vergiliae), which was the twelfth year of Domitian, the 7th day of Metroos according to the Bithynians. The date: 29 November, in the common year of Christ 92. Therefore the new moon of [Μητρῴου] Metroos fell on 23 November; and consequently the summer month on 26 July. It was the Iphitean year 868, and the 32nd of the twelfth Olympic period. In this period the summer month coincides three times with 26 July, in the years 13, 30, 47. But since the year in question was the fourth of an Olympiad, the Bithynian year too must have been either the fourth of the Bithynian tetraeteris, or the second: for otherwise the Olympic years are not employed in other periods. But no fourth year of the Olympic tetraeteris begins on 26 July. It will therefore be the 32nd year of the Olympic period, but the 30th

DE PERIODO BITHYNORUM.

Anno a Nabonassaro 840, Tybi 11 absoluto, Agrippa astrologus in Bithynia observavit Lunam iunctam cum australi succedente Vergiliarum, qui erat annus XII Domitiani, Metroi secundum Bithynos die VII. Tempus, XXIX Novembris, anno Christi vulgari 92: Ergo neomenia Μητρῴου congruebat vicesimae tertiae Novembris: et proinde mensis aestivus 26 Iulii. Erat annus Iphiteus 868, et periodi duodecimae Olympicae 32. In qua mensis aestivus ter competit vicesimae sextae Iulii, in annis 13, 30, 47. Sed cum annus propositus esset quartus Olympiadis, annum quoque Bithynicum aut quartum fuisse oportet Tetraeteridis Bithynicae, aut secundum: quod aliter anni Olympici non usurpantur in aliis periodis.
