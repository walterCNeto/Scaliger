Behold, we have constructed two tables on a double page, of the expanded and of the collected years. On the previous page is contained the quantity of the solar year [προεμπτώσεως σεληνιακῆς] "of the lunar proemptosis"; on the other, the difference between that year and the Julian, that is, [προέμπτωσις σεληνιακή] "lunar proemptosis". Rabbi Ada, or, as Epiphanius writes, [Αδδα] "Adda", seeing that in 600 years and more, from Callippus down to his own time, the computations of the Seleucid period were going from bad to worse, so that they began to delay by a full two days the epoch of the new moons—when Hipparchus's new moons were compared with the Callippic ones—discovered that this fault had been introduced through the use of the Julian year, which was longer than the true solar one. Wherefore, following Hipparchus, [he determined] that [quantity] of the sol[ar] year...
