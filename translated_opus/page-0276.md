## English

ON THE EMENDATION OF TIMES, BOOK III. 193

...of the Terms. This method would be straightforward if the epacts were precisely of whole days and did not have hours and scruples as appendages. Hence it follows that the days alone of the terms, with the hours and scruples excluded, do not over the course of time reach the limits of the new moon. For in 23 periods the Moon anticipates the cycle of the uniform year by one day. Therefore a correction must be applied whenever the sum of Egyptian, Armenian, or Persian years exceeds 500 years, or a little more. It will therefore be better to treat the method of the Moon by means of the character and the feria of the day. For this purpose we have prepared for you double Tables, in months, in expanded and collected years, through their respective periods. Their use is twofold, just as they themselves are twofold. For one may operate either through the anterior part or through the posterior part.

DE EMENDAT. TEMPORVM LIB. III. 193

Amum Terminorum. Haec ratio expedita erat, si epactae praecise essent dierum certorum, neque horas & scrupulos appendices haberent. Quo fit, vt dies tantum terminorum, exclusis horis, & scrupulis progressu temporis nouilunij fines non attingant. Nam in 23 periodis Luna anteuertit cyclum anni aequabilis die vno. Itaque castigatio adhibenda, quoties summa annorum Aegyptiorum, aut Armeniorum, aut Persicorum excedit 500 annos, aut paulo amplius. Melius igitur per characterem & feriam diei methodum Lunae tractabis. Cuius rei gratia Tabulas duplices tibi confecimus in mensibus, annis expansis & collectis per periodos suas. Cuius vsus duplex, vt & ipsa duplex. Aut enim per partem anteriorem, aut per posteriorem, operari
