## English

it is necessary to apply the period of 210 years to the method of the Moon. If this is true, they begin their Computation from the year of the confusion, from Nisan, whose new moon falls on 13 March, on a Friday (dies Veneris), which was the Goddess of the Hagarenes, as we have diligently shown elsewhere. Therefore this Nisan would be the last of the 210-year period. For from the year of the confusion to this year, which was 1584 of Christ, there are 1629 complete Julian years, which are 1679 Arabic solid years with 11 days in excess; and this year of the Samaritans was the Arabic year 1680 just beginning. Divided by 210, it leaves seven completed periods of 210 years, and the last year of the eighth period. And because this period began on a Friday, in the ferias of the new moons it differs in no way whatsoever from the period of the Hegira, in which the last year, namely the 210th, must begin on the first feria.

CONJUNCTION of Elhagiath) This conjunction is here noted on the second feria, with the third following, because it occurred after the tenth hour of the day. Therefore it belongs to the following night, from which the Samaritan civil day begins. But in the 210-year period, the last new moon ought to begin on the first feria, on the Kalends of March. For that reason they follow a better reckoning; but we could not ascertain the numbers themselves by any conjecture. It is said, however, to begin from 6 Adar Solar, whose new moon is 26 February, feria 5. Therefore it began from the night which followed the 5th day of Adar itself, after the second of Julian March, in solar cycle 25.

v. 31) That is, the first Adar Lunar begins on feria 5 on 26 February. The month has 31 days. So in what follows, the feria of the new moon of the Lunar month is appended, to which is subjoined the number of days of the Solar month.

And I am ignorant of the whole reckoning of these moments, as I said elsewhere, and of the syzygies, which in no way agrees with the mean motion of the Moon.

## Latim (transcrito)

rem periodum annorum 210 ad methodum Lunae adhibere necesse est. Quod si verum est, ordiuntur Computum suum ab anno confusionis, a Nisan, cuius neomenia incidit in XIII Martii, die Veneris, quae erat Hagarenorum Dea, ut alibi diligenter docuimus. Itaque hic Nisan fuerit ultimus periodi annorum 210. Nam ab anno confusionis, ad hunc annum, qui erat Christi 1584, sunt anni absoluti Iuliani 1629, qui sunt anni Arabici 1679 solidi cum XI diebus superfluis: & hic annus Samaritarum erat annus 1680 Arabicus iniens. Divisus per 210, relinquit septem periodos absolutas annorum 210, & ultimum annum octavae periodi. Et quia haec periodus caepit a die Veneris, in feriis neomeniarum nihil omnino differt a periodo Hegirae, in qua annus ultimus, nempe 210 debet incipere a feria prima.

CONIVNCTIO Elhagiathi) Haec coniunctio hic notatur feria secunda, sequente tertia, quia facta est post decimam horam diei. Itaque pertinet ad noctem sequentem, unde incipit dies civilis Samaritanus. Sed in periodo annorum 210, ultima neomenia debebat incipere feria prima, Kalendis Martii. Quare rationem meliorem sequuntur. sed numeros ipsos nulla coniectura assequi potuimus. Dicitur autem incipere a VI Adar Solaris scilicet, cuius neomenia XXVI Februarii, feria V. Ideo a nocte caepit, quae secuta est diem V ipsius Adar, post secundam Martii Iuliani, cyclo Solis XXV.

v. XXXI) Hoc est, prima Adar Lunaris incipit feria V in XXVI Februarii. Mensis habet XXXI dies. Sic in sequentibus feria neomeniae mensis Lunaris apposita est, cui subiungitur numerus dierum mensis Solaris.
