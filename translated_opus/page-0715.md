what need is there? Never did the ancient Greeks rave so much with the disease of mythologies as the Jews. When the reader thinks he has finished with some rabbinical matter [πραγματείᾳ], they crush him, against all expectation, precisely at the decisive point. For whenever a knot worthy of an untangler arises, at hand is the [Θεὸς ἀπὸ μηχανῆς] "god from the machine"—that is, some fable [πατροπαράδοτος] "handed down from the fathers," or some saying of an old rabbi, by which the whole business and dispute is settled. And why should the Moon be able to rival the Sun, seeing that she shines with her own light, she who without the Sun has no light at all? The first Jewish New Moon therefore occurred on the second day of the week (Monday), at hour 5. 204, after sunset, or—what is the same thing—on the second day of the week, at hour 11. 204, after noon at Jerusalem, in the year 953 of the Julian Period, on October 7, under the letter G, in the first solar cycle. For the Roman solar cycle is the same as the Jewish.

Solar Year) The second part of the Jewish Computus pertains to the Sun, and upon this part depends the doctrine of the Tekuphoth.

Excess of the Sun over the Lunar Year) Subtract the measure of the Lunar year 354. 8. 876 from the measure of the Solar year 365. 6. 0. There remains the solar excess [ὑπεροχὴ ἡλιακὴ] 10. 21. 204.

Excess of the Cycle) From the Canon of Gamaliel, which we expounded above, after the solar excess has been multiplied sevenfold and the lunar months subtracted, there remains the difference of Sun and Moon, hr. 1. 48. 5. This we demonstrated in the second book. Subtract seven synodic months 206. 17. 151 from the nineteen solar excesses, 206, 18. 636. There remains the same excess of the solar cycle, 0. 1. 485. This is the first Canon of the Tekuphoth.

Excess of the Solar Tohu) In the second book we indicated the cause of this excess. The Jewish Solar year was, and still is today, the same as the ancient Syro-Macedonian. For the tetraeteric Hyperberetaeus of the Alexandrian Macedonians began on October 8. Thus the last day of the year was October 7, when at the end of every fourth year a day was intercalated, both by the Syro-Macedonians and by the Hagarenes (Arabs) and the Jews, who used the Callippic Alexandrian period. Therefore the Tekuphah of autumn, which the Macedonians called [κέντρον μετοπωρινόν] "autumnal center," fell on October 7. The [κέντρον χειμερινόν] "winter center," the winter Tekuphah, on January 6. The vernal Tekuphah, [κέντρον ἐαρινόν] "spring center," on April 7. The summer Tekuphah, [κέντρον θερινόν] "summer center," on July 7. And these Tekuphoth are not a Jewish invention, but Greek, as we have said elsewhere, who called these parts of the year [ὥρας] "seasons." Once the Julian year had been published, the Antiochenes were the first of all to adopt that model at once, and then the Jews themselves. For Josephus too designates the times of the Jewish war by descriptions of the Julian days of Antioch: so that they may appear to have received it from the very beginning, at least from when the patrimony of Archelaus was reduced to the imperial treasury. ### Flags de incerteza

- A palavra grega 'πραγματείᾳ' está abreviada no original com sinal de abreviação; leitura provável mas não certa.
- Os números fracionários do tipo '5. 204', '8. 876', '1. 485' foram transcritos exatamente como no scan; seu significado exato (horas.partes) depende do sistema de Scaliger.
