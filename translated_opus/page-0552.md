SOBRE O PRIMEIRO JOGO NERONIANO.

Em moedas de Nero lê-se: JOGO QUINQUENAL INSTITUÍDO. O que não deve ser entendido como distinto daquele a que deu o nome de NERÔNIA: no qual Eusébio escreve que venceu com a cítara. Pois, à margem do ano IV da Olimpíada CCIX, escreve: 'Nero, disputando com a cítara em Roma, vence.' E mais abaixo, à margem do ano IV da Olimpíada CCX: 'Nero, disputando em Roma no certame dos citaristas, vence a todos.' Decorreram quatro anos inteiros: e por isso foi chamado de certame quinquenal. Cornélio Tácito, no Anal XIV, diz que foi instituído o primeiro certame neroniano quinquenal, sendo cônsules Nero pela quarta vez e Cornélio Cosso, os quais iniciaram a magistratura após Caio Vipsânio e Caio Fonteio, sob os quais demonstramos acima ter ocorrido um eclipse solar, na véspera das Calendas de maio, no ciclo lunar III. Portanto o primeiro Nerônia foi instituído no ciclo lunar IV; no ano de Cristo 60; e por conseguinte no quarto ano da Olimpíada CCIX. Com razão, pois, em Eusébio se registra que Nero venceu no mesmo ano com a cítara. O segundo lustro foi celebrado no último ano da Olimpíada seguinte, como já demonstramos a partir do mesmo Eusébio. No início do livro XVI dos Anais, Cornélio Tácito escreve: 'E por acaso o jogo quinquenal era celebrado no segundo lustro: e foi tomada pelos oradores como matéria especial em louvor do príncipe.' Se o primeiro lustro foi o ciclo quarto, então o segundo foi o ciclo oitavo, sendo cônsules C. Lecânio e M. Licínio. he seems to do also in the years of Caligula. We have treated these matters by our own method, although otherwise they can be handled most expeditiously by simply adding the years. Claudius died in Julian year 99, on the third day before the Ides of October, in lunar cycle XVII. Again, Nero was born in a Julian year, on the eighteenth day before the Kalends of January, in lunar cycle XIX. He perished in his thirty-second year, then current. To the years of Nero's age eighteen must be added, for the lunar method. Therefore he perished in year XII of the cycle, and consequently in Julian year 113. Now if you subtract 99 Julian years from 113, there will remain the complete Julian years during which he would have reigned, had he continued up to the third day before the Ides of October. This far we can safely affirm. For Suetonius kept silent about the day of death, though he writes that Nero took his own life on the same day on which he killed his wife Octavia. If we believe Xiphilinus, Nero perished on the 9th of July, in the year of Christ 68. For from the death of Nero to the beginning of Vespasian—that is, to the Kalends of July in the year of Christ 69—he says a full year and 22 days intervened. We embrace this opinion, and do not doubt it is true: especially since Cassiodorus writes that he reigned 13 years, 7 months, and 28 days, a time which ends on the 9th of June. Therefore there is no doubt that Nero perished on the fifth day before the Ides of June, and that he was wrongly omitted by Suetonius.

ON THE FIRST NERONIAN GAMES.

On coins of Nero one reads: QUINQUENNIAL CONTEST INSTITUTED. This is not to be understood as distinct from the one to which he gave the name NERONIA: in which Eusebius writes that he won on the cithara. For opposite year IV of Olympiad CCIX he writes: 'Nero, competing at Rome with the cithara, prevails.' And further below, opposite year IV of Olympiad CCX: 'Nero, competing at Rome in the contest of citharists, overcomes all.' Four full years intervened: and therefore the contest was called quinquennial. Cornelius Tacitus, in Annals XIV, says that the first Neronian quinquennial contest was instituted when Nero (for the fourth time) and Cornelius Cossus were consuls, who entered the magistracy after Gaius Vipsanius and Gaius Fonteius, under whom we have demonstrated above that a solar eclipse occurred, on the day before the Kalends of May, in lunar cycle III. Therefore the first Neronia was instituted in lunar cycle IV; in the year of Christ 60; and consequently in the fourth year of Olympiad CCIX. Rightly, then, in Eusebius it is noted that Nero won on the cithara in that same year. The second lustrum was celebrated in the last year of the following Olympiad, as we have already demonstrated from the same Eusebius. Near the beginning of Annal XVI, Cornelius Tacitus writes: 'And it happened that the quinquennial games were celebrated in the second lustrum: and this was taken up by the orators as a special theme in praise of the emperor.' If the first lustrum was cycle four, then the second was cycle eight, when C. Lecanius and M. Licinius were consuls. DE PRIMO AGONE NERONIANO.

In numismatis Neronis legitur: CERTAMEN QVINQVENNALE INSTITVTVM. quod non aliud intelligendum ab eo, cui nomen fecit NERONIA: in quo Eusebius scribit cithara vicisse. E regione enim anni IIII Olymp. CCIX scribit: Nero Romae cithara contendens superat. Et infra e regione anni IIII Olympiad. CCX. Nero Romae in citharistarum agone contendens omnes superat. Quatuor anni solidi interiecti sunt: & propterea quinquennale certamen appellatum. Cor. Tacitus Annali XIIII, ait primum certamen Neronianum quinquennale institutum, Nerone quartum, Cornelio Cosso coss. qui inierunt magistratum post C. Vipsanium, & C. Fonteium, sub quibus eclipsim Solis contigisse supra demonstrauimus, prid. Kal. Mai, cyclo Lunae III. Secundum lustrum celebratum anno vltimo sequentis Olympiadis, ex eodem Eusebio iam demonstrauimus. Circa initium annalis XVI, Cor. Tacitus scribit: Ac forte quinquennale ludicrum secundo lustro celebrabatur: oratoribusque praecipua materia in laudem principis assumpta est.
