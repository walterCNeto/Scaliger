Since therefore [the year] begins at Spring, not from the preceding Autumn, a unit must always be added to the Alexandrian years; from the remainder, all periods of 228 years are to be deducted. The current years of the Hegirian period, accommodated to the Julian new moons, will remain. If these years are fewer than 77, they belong to the first Callippic period; if fewer than 153, with 76 subtracted, the rest will belong to the second period. If finally they are more than 153, with 152 subtracted, the rest belong to the third Callippic period, as you plainly see in the Table. The year 622 of Christ was the 933rd Alexandrian. Adding the unit, and discarding all the 228s, there remains year XXI of the Julian Hegirian period, composed of three times 76 years, that is, of three Callippic periods. These 21 years, being within 77, belong to the first Callippic period. Its first month Ramadhan falls on March 20, weekday VII (Saturday), solar cycle XV. Therefore the following Muharram is on weekday VI (Friday), on July 16. Hence begins the first year of the Muhammadan Hegira; in year 5335 of the Julian period; in the year of the World 4571, Karkinonos 27, Zygon 2.12.45; September 21, in the Jewish year 4382; in the third [era], whose character is 4.6.112, July 14; in the year of Nabonassar 1370, Choiac 28; in Julian year 667; in year 71 of the Armenians, Sahami 25. As for what some write, that the day of Venus (Friday) was sanctified as sacred by Muhammad because the first Muharram of the Hegira has the character of weekday VI, they are mistaken in vain. For Friday is no more instituted by Muhammad than circumcision. Both, indeed, are most ancient among that people. Concerning this matter, hear the words of a certain Christian, from his book against Muhammadism, as the author of Beauvais cites them: "Of two peoples of India, who are called ZECHIAM and ALBARACHUMA [البرجمي BRACHMANI], there was an ancient custom, naked and shaven, with great ululations resounding, to walk around the images of Demons, also to kiss the corners, and to throw stones onto a heap, which was erected as if in honor of the gods. For this reason it is said in the book of Solomon: 'He who throws a stone onto a heap of Mercury.' They did this twice a year, namely with the Sun being in the first degree of Aries, and again when it was in the first degree of Libra: that is, at the beginning of Spring and of Autumn. This custom, therefore, having descended from the Indians to the Arabs, and Muhammad having found it in his time being celebrated at Mecca in honor of Venus, he ordered it to remain thus, although he had removed the other traces of Idolatry. But it is said that to Venus alone, in that celebration, it was customary to offer, that the little stones be thrown backward, that is, under the genital members; because Venus dominates most especially over those parts."

### Flags de incerteza

- Transliteração árabe 'البرجمي' (Brachmani): leitura do árabe impresso pouco nítida; reproduzido conforme aparenta; possível variante de grafia.
- A forma grega 'προέμπτωσιν σεληναίου' está preservada como impressa; o acento e a forma final de σεληναίου podem divergir levemente do padrão clássico.
- 'Zygon 2.12.45' — sequência numérica copiada conforme o original; interpretação dos separadores incerta.
- 'Ab tertia, cuius character 4.6.112' — sequência numérica preservada; 112 pode ser lida alternativamente.


### Notas do tradutor

Página trata da conversão entre o calendário alexandrino, os períodos calípicos e o início da era da Hégira (16 de julho de 622 d.C.). Scaliger refuta a tradição de que Maomé teria instituído a sexta-feira como dia sagrado devido ao carácter do primeiro Muharram, e cita Vincentius Bellovacensis (Vincent de Beauvais) sobre o costume indiano adotado em Meca.
