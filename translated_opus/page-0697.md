## English

occurred in the year 480 of Christ, in solar cycle XIII. For it does not appear to have been in the year 486, solar cycle XIX. The time is too remote. On a loose sheet, therefore, the Epitaph was thus written, which our friend P. Pithou, a man of the highest probity and erudition, communicated to us:

OF C. SOLLIUS APOLLINARIS MODESTUS SIDONIUS.

Neighbor to the saints, and to his sacred father,
here by his merits lives Apollinaris,
illustrious in titles, powerful in honor,
commander of the army and judge of the forum,
serene amid the swelling waves of the world,
tempering at times the motions of affairs,
he gave laws to barbarian fury.
To kingdoms at discord in the midst of arms
he restored peace by his ample counsel.
Among these things, nonetheless, philosophizing,
he wrote works to be preserved for unending ages.
And after such losses, by grace seated
on the chair of the Supreme Pontiff,
he returned worldly affairs to his offspring.
Whoever here, amid tears, shall pray to God,
pour forth auspicious prayers over the sepulcher.
Unknown to none, and to be read by the world,
may Sidonius there be invoked by you.

XII Kal. September, Zeno being Emperor. He places [the death] on the XII Kalends of September, although the Church of Auvergne from the most ancient times has held to X Kalends. Otherwise, if he had died on the XII Kalends, this would have been in the year of Christ 482, the seventh of Zeno's reign. The memory of so great a man was therefore not to be omitted, especially since this notation continues the series of times of the empire of the Franks. Sidonius therefore died in the fifth year of Zeno Augustus, after the consulship of Zeno himself and of Flavius Basilius the younger, vir clarissimus and consul, in the second year of Clovis, as will be shown hereafter, in the third indiction, on the X Kalends of September, a Saturday. From the death of Martin to the death of Clovis II, whom he calls Hlodoueus, he reckons 112 years; a number which appears not in figures but expressed in words in Ado of Vienne, so that all suspicion of error is removed. Now Martin died in the year of Christ 395, as was demonstrated above. Therefore Clovis, or Hldovvin II, died in the year 507. But he himself supposes the death of Martin to have been from the year 397, under the consuls Atticus and Caesarius. By this reckoning, Clovis would have died in the year of Christ 509. This must be demonstrated. In the third Council of Orléans —

## Latim (transcrito)

contigit anno 480 Christi, cyclo Solis XIII. Nam non videtur anno 486, cyclo Solis XIX. Nimis enim remotum est tempus. In schedo igitur ita scriptum fuit Epitaphium, quod P. Pithoeus noster, vir summa & probitate & eruditione, nobis communicauit:

C. SOLLII APOLLINARIS MODESTI SIDONII.

Sanctis contiguus, sacroque Patri,
Viuit hic meritis Apollinaris,
Inlustris titulis, potens honore,
Rector militiae, forique iudex,
Mundi inter tumidas quietus vndas,
Causarum moderans subinde motus
Leges barbarico dedit furori.
Discordantibus inter arma regnis
Pacem consilio reduxit amplo.
Haec inter tamen & philosophando
Scripsit perpetuis habenda saeclis.
Et post talia damna gratiarum
Summi Pontificis sedens cathedram
Mundanos soboli refundit actus.
Quisque hic, dum lacrimis Deum rogabis,
Dextrum funde preces super sepulcrum.
Nulli incognitus, & legendus orbi
Illic Sidonius tibi inuocetur.

XII. Kal. Sept. Zenone Imperatore. XII Kal. Sept. ponit, cum Ecclesia Aruernorum a prima vsque antiquitate retineat X Kal. Alioqui si obierit XII Kal. hoc fuerit anno Christi quidem 482, imperij autem Zenoniani septimo. Non igitur omittenda erat tanti viri memoria, praesertim cum haec notatio continuet seriem temporum imperij Francorum. Obiit igitur Sidonius anno V. Zenonis Augusti, post consulatum ipsius Zenonis, Fl. Basilio iuniore V.C. COS. anno secundo Hlodouei, vt postea demonstrabitur, indictione tertia, X Kal. Sept. Sabbato. Ab excessu Martini, ad excessum Hlodvvin secundi, quem Hlodoueum vocat, annos putat CXII. qui etiam numerus non notis, sed verbis conceptus extat apud Adonem Viennensem, vt omnis suspitio erroris absit. Martinus autem obiit anno Christi CCCXCV, vt supra demonstratum est. Ergo Hlodoueus, siue Hldovvin secundus obiit anno DVII. Sed ipse putat obitum Martini ab anno CCCXCVII, Attico, & Caesareo COSS. Hoc pacto Hlodoueus obierit anno Christi DVIIII. Hoc demonstrandum est. In tertio Concilio Aurelia-


### Notas do tradutor
