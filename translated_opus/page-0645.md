SKIRMISHES OF THE ANCIENTS CONCERNING THE DAY OF THE LORD'S PASSION.

But we are moved aside by the authorities of the ancients, whose judgment, because of their learning, sanctity, and antiquity, must be taken into account. Yet what standard can be held, when one says one thing and another another? Christ suffered on the 14th of Nisan, on a Friday: no sane person will deny this. From April of Tiberius's fifteenth year to April of his nineteenth, there are five Passovers: of which only two have the first day of Unleavened Bread on a Friday, namely, in the sixteenth year and in the nineteenth year of Tiberius's reign. The sixteenth year of Tiberius was the second year from the baptism, as we have already seen, whose Nisan fell on March 25, a Saturday. Accordingly, the fourteenth of Nisan, or [πρώτη ἀζύμων] "the first day of Unleavened Bread," was April 7, a Friday. Some of the ancients therefore write that Christ preached for only one year, in the fifteenth year of Tiberius, and was crucified in the sixteenth. This was the most ancient opinion of all. Clement: [τ', τε πάθος αὐτοῦ ἀκριβολογούμενοι ἀναφέρουσιν καὶ μήν τινες τῷ ἐκ καὶ δέκατῳ ἔτει τοῦ Τιβερίου Καίσαρος, Φαμενὼθ κε καὶ ᾗ Φαρμουθὶ κε. ἄλλοι ᾗ Φαρμουθὶ ιθ πεπονθέναι τὸν σωτῆρα λέγουσι] "And some, calculating his passion with precision, record it; and certain ones place it in the fifteenth year of Tiberius Caesar, on the 25th of Phamenoth and on the 25th of Pharmuthi; but others say that the Savior suffered on the 19th of Pharmuthi." The 25th of Phamenoth is March 22. It is so far from Christ having suffered on that day, that this could not have happened in that century except in the lunar cycle VIII, that is, in the twelfth year of Tiberius.
