## English

the third, neither a common nor an embolismic [year] is ever deficient, nor is a common one ever abundant. When the weekday is the second, a common or embolismic [year] is never ordinary, just as when the weekday is the seventh. Otherwise Nisan would fall on weekdays 2, 4, 6. This period which we have proposed, when we were speaking of the Syromacedonian year, is not only useful for marking Jewish years, but also without it you will never be able to identify them. The Jews used it down to the times of Constantine the Great, and even beyond those times, when the Moon had already anticipated the old epoch by two days. And this epoch they call the era of the contracts, or [מנין שטרות] "reckoning of documents." Now it is time to test its reliability. First, then, concerning the transposition of the characterism of Tisri, which certain Rabbis say was devised after the edition of the Talmud, and some of our own have persuaded themselves of the same, reason entirely escapes both parties. For it was used anciently and under the Seleucids. In the year of the Lord's Passion the neomenia of Nisan fell on Saturday: although its characterism by today's computation is 5.19.95, the sixth weekday. But this must be demonstrated from the reliability of our Seleucid period. It was the year of the Contracts, or Alexandrian, 344: and therefore the fortieth year of the fifth period. Tisri [falls on] September 24, the fourth weekday: which, changed to the fifth in the ordinary common year, gives the seventh weekday for the neomenia of Nisan. Again, in the vulgar year of Christ 70, the neomenia of Nisan was on the Kalends of April, as Josephus attests. For the fourteenth of Nisan coincided with the fourteenth of the Julian month. It was the first year of the sixth period of the Contracts, Tisri on September 6, the fourth weekday, solar cycle 22. Therefore, changed to the fifth, combined with the characterism of the weekday of Nisan in a deficient embolismic year, it will give the first weekday of the neomenia of Nisan on the Kalends of April, solar cycle 23. In both examples, then, the transposition of the weekday was calendrical. Indeed, you could never find that neomenia from the present computation. For, as the Jews today suppose, the characterism of the neomenia of Nisan would have been 6.19.409, Saturday, the last of March. If they had used this computation in those times, the neomenia of Nisan, as you see, would have fallen on the seventh weekday, not, as Josephus truthfully writes, on the first weekday. But let us fortify the reliability of this most excellent period with still more arguments. Josephus writes that before the beginning of the Jewish War, that is, around the vulgar year of Christ 67, more or less, many portents were seen: among which he relates wonderful things about a certain fanatic; which you may seek from him: and that this happened at the time when the Passover, that is, the fourteenth of Nisan, coincided with April 8. This could not have happened except when the lunar cycle was 9, in the year of Christ 65, two years before the beginning of the Jewish War. Which wonderfully agrees with Josephus's thinking. Nunc tempus est, ut eius fidem periclitemur. Primum igitur de translatione characterismi Tisri, quod quidam Rabbini dicunt post Thalmudis editionem excogitatum esse, & quidam ex nostris sibi persuaserunt, omnino utrosque ratio fugit.
