For he saw that the quarter of a day had been unjustly left out by Meton, and that 27,760 days, which for Meton constituted seventy-six years, exceeded the same number of solar years by one day: so that the true 76 solar years amount to 27,759 days. But the 19 Greek Tetraeterides, with nine full intercalary months, come to 27,763 days: from which, if 27,759 days are subtracted, there remain four days to be removed beyond the 19 ordinary [ἐξαιρέσιμοι] "days to be removed." Now 76 Greek years are 27,360 days, to which, if you add the 270 days of the nine intercalary months, the total becomes 27,630 days: which, subtracted from the 27,759 days, leave [ἀνάρχους] "without proper assignment" the days we have called appendices, namely 129. When these 129 are distributed over 76 years, all 19 Tetraeterides will indeed be [ἐξαιρεσιμαῖοι] "of removal," but four among them will be [διεξαιρεσιμαῖοι] "of double removal," namely: the first, fifth, tenth, and fifteenth—that is, the fourth, twentieth, fortieth, and sixtieth years will be [διεξαιρεσιμαῖοι]—so that the new moons of the first month of the Tetraeterides agree wonderfully: and indeed this scheme is most elegant, given such ignorance of the Lunar motion. Anyone who wishes to test it can verify this. Callippus, for his part, innovated nothing in this matter, but, as we said, showed only that in nineteen Tetraeterides four [ἐξαιρέσεις] "removals" occur beyond the 19 ordinary ones, so that 23 [ἐξαιρέσεις] "removals" correspond to that same number of Tetraeterides. And this period was embraced not only by the Athenians, but also by other Greek nations. The oldest, however, of all Tetraeterides is the Olympic. ### Notas do tradutor

A citação poética é de Estácio, Silvas I (sobre a villa de Manílio Vopisco ou similar): 'domus improba frangit frigora; Pisaeumque domus non aestuat annum' — referência ao ano Olímpico (Pisaeum annum = ano de Pisa/Olímpia). 'Neomenia Ab Iudaici' refere-se provavelmente à neomenia segundo a era judaica / computus hebraico (Scaliger usa 'Ab Iudaici' como termo técnico). O 'Character 3.3.905' é notação cronológica scaligeriana específica.
