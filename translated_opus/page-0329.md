## English

the intercalary [day], which we call Bissextile, was inserted in that place where the embolismic lunar month is also inserted, namely before Tishri, although it was not called Tishri but the first month. Then, once the spring month was taken as the head of the year, the bissextile was transferred to springtime, because before spring also the intercalation of the nearest lunar month was established. But after they received from the Seleucids the form of the year and the Callippic lunar period, nothing was changed in the reckoning of the solar year. For their Bissextile was inserted between the first and second of April: which was the Tekupha of Nisan. Afterwards, once the Julian form was adopted, the Bissextile began to be intercalated between the 25th and 26th of March, which method has prevailed among them to this day. And because from March 25 to April 2, which was the seat of the ancient bissextile, there remain 7 days, for this reason the same number of days is today subtracted to investigate the Tekupha of Nisan, just as thirteen are subtracted whenever the Tekupha of Tishri is investigated. They call Tekupha the quarter of the Julian year, which consists of 91 days and 7 ½ hours. In the times of Moses, only a single Tekupha was spoken of, as a point interposed between the end of the preceding year and the head of the following: namely in autumn — around which time, as closely as possible, God commands [σκηνοπηγίαν] "the feast of tabernacles" to be celebrated. The Tekupha, therefore, is a moment, or point of time, at which, when one quarter of the year is finished, another quarter immediately begins. They observe this point so superstitiously that, were they to exceed it by even the breadth of a hair, they would think themselves to be entering upon great danger of life. For to each Tekupha they assign its own element. For example, to the Tekupha of Tammuz, the solstitial one, they attribute fire. From the excess of the elements they suppose danger to threaten things: for example, from the Tekupha of Tammuz [ἐκπύρωσιν] "a conflagration": and whoever should drink or eat at the moment of that Tekupha would be seized by a most burning fever. I have here appended a Table of all the Tekuphas of one quadrennium: which, once the quadrennium is completed, return in a cycle. By dividing the Jewish year by four, you will easily know to which year each Tekupha belongs. For if after the division a remainder of one is left, the Tekupha of Tishri is 9. 0; if two, the Tekupha of Tishri is 3. 0. And so on. When the hour is below 12, it falls in the night; when greater, in the day: and the 12 hours must be discarded. The Tekuphas alone are fixed and stable in the Jewish Computus, inasmuch as they are Solar. ### Notas do tradutor

Página discute a Tekupha (תקופה) judaica: os quatro pontos cardinais do ano solar (equinócios e solstícios) conforme o cômputo rabínico, com tábua de valores para um ciclo quadrienal.
