## English

a man more learned than any the Orient has produced in our age: [Arabic text] That is: "Furthermore, you asked about the ERA OF MARTYRS used by the Egyptians, what it is, and for what reason it is so called. Know that its beginning is from the nineteenth year of the impious King Diocletian [ἀθέου, 'godless'], in which he intensified the persecution against the Christians, having decreed that their churches be destroyed, and likewise that whoever did not sacrifice to the idols should be killed. On account of this edict were martyred [ἐμαρτυρίωθησαν] one hundred forty-four thousand seven hundred men of the faithful of the land of Egypt. From that time begins the Era Elkupti, which they call the ERA OF MARTYRS, namely of those whose blood Diocletian shed." Thus that eminent man. It is therefore an equal abuse to call "era of martyrs" one that does not begin in the first year of the persecution, and to attribute to Diocletian what precedes its beginning by three years. This era was always entirely that of the Christians, and still is today, and belongs solely to Egypt and Ethiopia. For the ancients never reckon years from Diocletian without mentioning the Egyptian months: as in Theon, in the sixth book of the Commentary on Ptolemy, page 284, Lunar Eclipse in the year 81 of Diocletian, on Athyr 29: and of Nabonassar 1112, Phamenoth 6. And in the Acts of the Council of Ephesus, 292: [Ἀνεγνώσθη ἐν τῇ ἐκκλησίᾳ τῶν μοναζόντων ἐν τοῖς ἐρημικοῖς, Φαρμουθὶ κγ. ἰνδικτιῶνι δ. α. ἔτους Διοκλητιανοῦ ρξδ.] "It was read in the church of the monks in the desert places, on Pharmuthi 23, indiction 4, year 1 [sic], in the 164th year of Diocletian." And this year was A.D. 448, April 17. But the eclipse of Theon falls in the year of Christ 364, which was the first of Valentinian, November 25, Thursday, in the year 5077 of the Julian Period, solar cycle 9, lunar 4, fourth of Toxonos, year 4314 from the foundation of the world. ### Flags de incerteza

- Transliteração árabe feita por leitura visual; pequenos detalhes diacríticos podem variar.
- Forma grega 'ἐμαρτυρίωθησαν' preservada como impressa, embora a forma clássica esperada seja ἐμαρτυρήθησαν.
- No trecho grego do Concílio de Éfeso, a leitura 'α. ἔτους' (ano 1 / ano) é sintaticamente estranha; mantida conforme impresso.


### Notas do tradutor

A página discute a Era dos Mártires (Era Diocletiana) a partir de citação árabe de um autor oriental (provavelmente al-Makin ou semelhante), seguida de tradução latina de Scaliger. Scaliger argumenta que a era não começa no primeiro ano da perseguição, mas três anos antes (ascensão de Diocleciano). A seguir cita um eclipse lunar registrado por Teão com dupla datação (era de Diocleciano e era de Nabonassar), e uma ata do Concílio de Éfeso datada pelo ano 164 de Diocleciano = 448 d.C.
