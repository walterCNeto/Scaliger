if so many arguments are not enough to persuade of the truth, it is madness to deal with such people. From the first year of Cyrus to the last of Darius, the work of the temple was disrupted. Ezra IV, 5. From the beginning of Ahasuerus, who is Xerxes, throughout the entire time of Ahasuerus himself and of Artaxerxes, until the second year of Darius, no longer by interposing delays, but by assailing the Jews with slanders before kings Xerxes and Artaxerxes, their enemies not only hindered the work but also brought the Jews into the gravest danger. Ezra IV, 6, 7, 11, 12, 13, and following. Concerning this matter, in the second year of Darius, the Angel complains to Zechariah: "O Lord God of hosts, how long wilt thou not have mercy on Jerusalem and on the cities of Judah, against which thou art angry? Behold, this is now the seventieth year!" Here is the disruption of seventy years, which the enemies of truth cannot contradict. Will they say that that Darius is the son of Hystaspes? The second year of Darius in the Julian period is 4194. Subtracting seventy, there remains the year 4124, that is, the third year of the 47th Olympiad, in which Zedekiah was captured. Which is absurd. Therefore Darius, under whom Zechariah prophesied, is other than the son of Hystaspes. He is therefore Darius Nothus. Ezra goes from Xerxes to the second year of Darius, IV, 24. And after that Darius, another Artaxerxes, VII, 1. Who is that Artaxerxes after Xerxes, besides Artaxerxes Longimanus? Who is the Darius after that Artaxerxes and before another Artaxerxes, other than Darius Nothus, the middle one between the two Artaxerxes, Longimanus and Mnemon? The words of Ezra must first be refuted, before my opinion is attacked. Ezra must first be convicted of falsehood, before I am. Or if I am a liar, then I lie along with Ezra. Let them deny that this stands thus in Ezra. I yield. How inept they are, how malicious, who deny these things! with how much hatred of truth they do so! Referência bíblica a Zacarias 1,12 (citação grifada em itálico no original). O argumento cronológico: o 2º ano de Dario situado no ano 4194 do Período Juliano; subtraindo 70, resulta 4124 = 3º ano da 47ª Olimpíada (ano da captura de Sedecias).
